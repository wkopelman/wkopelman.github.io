Television Reporting Coverage

My experience in covering Division I college athletics and local news for a television audience ranges across an array of assignments. From a profile on Miami men's basketball's Final Four coach to the tradition of a military-grade obstacle course, "Soldier Rush" on Veteran's Day, I have prioritized the value of timely reporting and story production for UMTV and look to generate this in a professional atmosphere. This fall, I also shot, edited and produced a 13-minute short documentary of Miami basketball's first-ever Final Four run. It was more than just another challenge, but a dream come true.

How my passion for covering sports started

Growing up in New York City, I was surrounded by some of the most storied teams in professional sports, and behind them, highly passionate fanbases with minute levels of patience and what felt like dozens of the industry's finest team network broadcasters and writers. Whether it involved finding a column in the New York Post on the playoff futility of the New York Knicks, or turning the radio dial for talk on the growth of Eli Manning's unshakable legacy, understanding the immense pressure — and even at times, doubt — from New York's fanbases didn't require much perusing. But upon my decision to leave the northeast for Coral Gables, the importance of consistency in college athletics coverage for an award-winning college TV station galvanized me into upholding the same work ethic I observed from other peers. In a setting valued around industry-standard story coverage, I soon learned the decision I had made was the most beneficial I had made in my life, before I had even decided to stay to pursue a master's degree.
